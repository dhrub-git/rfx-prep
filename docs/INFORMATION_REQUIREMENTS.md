## Information Architecture Overview

### Data Classification Framework
Information is classified into four primary categories based on usage frequency, sensitivity, and maintenance requirements:

#### Tier 1: Core Company Data (Static/Infrequent Updates)
- Legal and corporate structure
- Fundamental business information
- Core certifications and licenses
- Foundational policies and procedures

#### Tier 2: Operational Information (Semi-Dynamic)
- Technical capabilities and infrastructure
- Service delivery methodologies
- Quality management systems
- Performance metrics and KPIs

#### Tier 3: Project-Specific Data (Dynamic)
- Past performance case studies
- Team assignments and availability
- Customer references and relationships
- Pricing models and commercial terms

#### Tier 4: Market Intelligence (Highly Dynamic)
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Industry trends and regulations
- Customer-specific intelligence
- Market pricing benchmarks

### Information Quality Standards
All information must meet the following quality criteria:

#### Accuracy Requirements
- **Primary Sources:** Information must be traceable to authoritative sources
- **Verification:** All data requires dual verification before publication
- **Currency:** Maximum age limits defined by information type
- **Completeness:** Minimum required fields specified for each data category

#### Accessibility Standards
- **Searchability:** All information must be tagged and indexed
- **Retrievability:** Response time < 3 seconds for standard queries
- **Usability:** Information presented in proposal-ready formats
- **Permissions:** Role-based access controls implemented
